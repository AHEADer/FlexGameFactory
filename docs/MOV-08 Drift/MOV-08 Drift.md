##  MOV-08 Drift
    
![figure_197_232_873_717](https://tosv.byted.org/obj/labcv-ocr/Seed_PDF_figures/figure_2024_10_19_17_7_23_693_197_232_873_717.jpg)
    
###  Description
 Two movement cards are played to move. The sum represents the distance forward moved; the difference represents the sideways movement (Drift).    
###  Discussion
 First introduced in the dog-sled racing game, Snow Tail, this Drift system is both thematic and gives players options for dlever play. Each of the two dogs pulling the sled has a number card placed on it. The sled is moved forward by the sum of the numbers, and it must change lanes equal to the difference, in the direction of the dog that was assigned the higher number.    
 Since players may only play one card per turn (with the card on the other dog remaining the same), careful management of resource, and how you Drift through the course, is essential for winning play.    
 While this system has not been used outside of Snow Tails and its successor Mush! Mush: Snow Tail 2, we feel that it is worth including here because it shows how different movement mechanisms can be thematic, clever, and straightforward. It may well be that the close consonance between theme and mechanism is precisely why this hasn't been reused.

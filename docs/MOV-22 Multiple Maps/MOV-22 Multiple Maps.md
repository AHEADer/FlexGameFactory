##  MOV-22 Multiple Maps
    
![figure_185_233_883_598](https://tosv.byted.org/obj/labcv-ocr/Seed_PDF_figures/figure_2024_10_19_17_7_54_883_185_233_883_598.jpg)
    
###  Description
 The game takes place on Muliple Maps which are connected at defined points.    
##  Discussion
 This mechanism is implemented in Iron Dragon, a rail game set in a fantasy world. Trains may run through the main board, or transition to a special Underground map. The two are connected at specific points, which can make for interesting route building.    
 Khronos has multiple boards, but these are connected in time rather than space. They represent three different ages of the same region, and actions that are taken on older boards, like building structures, can ripple through into the future boards.    
 Fische Fluppen Frikadellen is a trading game that may be played by up to15 players on three maps placed at separate places in the room. Each board is played individually--turns are not synced between boards-and players may move from board to board under certain circumstances, taking their goods with them in an attempt to make better deals. When arriving at a new board, they are inserted into the turn order. Players move around each map visiting individual stores for buying and selling, and prices fluctuate independently.    
 Having Multiple Maps forces players to approach the eometry of the situation in a different way. It may present shortcuts or advantages that need    
 to be evaluated in the context of the cost to take advantage of them. It also allows the designers to create more complex structures.
